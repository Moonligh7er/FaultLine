Reporting

Several ways to tell the world about a problem — designed so that even a 30-second window between meetings is enough.

⚡ Quick Report (10 seconds)

Tap the "+" button, pick what you're reporting, snap a photo. The app fills in your location automatically and sends it. That's it. Built for people who don't have time for forms.

📋 Detailed Report (when you want to be thorough)

A guided five-step wizard for when you want to add multiple photos, drag the location pin to the exact spot, and rate the severity in detail. Same idea, more polish.

🗣️ Voice Commands

Say "Report pothole" or "Fix this" out loud and the app starts a report — hands-free. Useful while driving when you can't safely fumble with a phone.

🗨️ Audio-Guided Mode

The app talks you through the whole thing and listens to your answers. You never have to look at the screen. Designed for visually impaired users and anyone using the app while moving.

📍 GPS + Drag-to-Adjust Pin

Your phone knows roughly where you are. The app drops a pin on a map, and you can drag it to the exact spot. The address fills in by itself.

🤖 AI Looks at Your Photo

After you take a picture, an AI checks it: "That looks like a medium pothole, about 18 inches across, on a paved road." It pre-fills the form with what it sees so you barely have to type.

📷 Smart Photo Coaching

The camera shows simple tips while you're aiming: "Hold a shoe next to it for scale" or "Photograph from about 3 feet away." Your reports come out clearer; the AI gets more accurate.

📳 Bump Detection (while driving)

If you turn it on, the app notices when your car hits something hard. It asks "Was that a pothole?" so you can confirm and report it without taking your hands off the wheel.

👤 Anonymous Reporting

You don't need an account. You can report without ever telling us who you are. Your name and email are never attached unless you choose.

📶 Works Offline

No signal? No problem. Reports save on your phone and send themselves the moment you reconnect. Subway commuters, rural areas, parking garages — all fine.

🏷️ 24 Categories

Not just potholes. Streetlights, sidewalks, signage, crosswalks, water mains, sewers, fallen trees, snow and ice, bike lanes, abandoned vehicles, illegal dumping, parks, and more.

📊 Smart Severity Questions

The questions adapt to what you're reporting. Potholes get asked about size and danger. Streetlights get asked about urgency. You never see questions that don't apply.

👥
Community Verification

One person yelling about a pothole is easy to ignore. Ten people pointing at the same one isn't.

3️⃣ Three Reports = Verified

When three different people report the same issue at the same place, it's marked "community-verified." This filters out one-off complaints and confirms real problems.

🔔 Witness Network

If someone reports a dangerous hazard near you, the app may ping you: "Can you confirm? It's 200m from where you are." If you're heading that way anyway, one tap and a photo helps.

👍 Upvote & Confirm

"Upvote" means "this matters." "Confirm" means "I've seen it too." Both push the report higher in priority. Cities pay attention to volume.

🔗 Duplicate Detection

About to report something that already has eight reports on it? The app notices and asks "Add yours to this one?" — so you're not creating extra noise, you're amplifying a real signal.

📢 Community Campaigns

Rally the neighborhood. Create a campaign called "Fix Elm Street," set a goal, and watch progress. Turns scattered complaints into organized momentum.

📨
Authority Escalation

Once enough people care, the app does the boring paperwork and contacts the right person at the right department for you.

🏛️ Knows Who To Email

The app knows your local government boundaries. It figures out whether your issue belongs to the city, the county, or the state — and who at that level to contact. 42 authorities in MA, RI, and NH are pre-loaded.

📧 Auto-Escalation

When ten reports about the same problem pile up over 30 days, the app sends a professional email to the responsible authority with photos, GPS data, fiscal projections, and community testimonials. You don't have to do anything.

🔌 Direct Submission for Some Cities

Boston, Cambridge, and hundreds of other cities have systems Fault Line can talk to directly. Reports go straight into their ticket queues with real tracking numbers — no email needed.

🔄 Status Updates Come Back

For those connected cities, when they mark something "fixed" in their system, your app updates automatically. You'll know when it's done.

🚨 Emergency Fast-Track

Reports rated "extremely dangerous" skip the normal threshold. They're flagged immediately and prioritized for the fastest possible escalation.

⚖️
Accountability & Legal Tools

Tools that turn "this is annoying" into "this is going to cost the city money if they don't fix it."

📜 Legal Demand Letters

Tap one button and the app generates a formal legal notice citing your state's actual statute. It tracks the legal deadline. You can email it directly to the right office.

🛡️ Insurance Claim Package

If you've already hit the pothole and need to file a claim, one tap creates a complete evidence bundle: timestamped GPS, photos, the city's notification history, and community confirmation count. Send it to your insurance.

💰 Fiscal Impact Math

The app shows authorities the numbers: "Fix this for $75 today, or face $12,000 in liability next year." We do the math. Inaction becomes the expensive option.

⏱️ Public Response Countdown

The clock starts when the city is notified. Everyone can see how long they're taking. Miss the deadline? Everyone who reported it gets a notification.

🏗️ Track the Contractors

If the same pothole was "fixed" by Contractor X and reopens three months later, Fault Line records that. Their failure rate becomes public.

🔍 Repair Quality Check

After a fix, nearby users photograph the result. The AI grades it: Excellent, Good, Adequate, Poor, or Failed. Bad work re-opens the report automatically.

📸 Deterioration Timelapse

If the same spot is photographed over weeks or months, the app stitches the photos into a visual timeline of neglect. Powerful when shared on social media.

🎥 Video Testimonials

Record a 15-second video about how the issue affects your life. It gets attached to escalation emails. Spreadsheets are easy to ignore. Faces aren't.

📊
Data & Transparency

Public data nobody can hide from. Comparative pressure between cities is the most powerful force in local politics.

🏆 Infrastructure Health Grades

Every city gets a public letter grade — A through F — based on how many issues there are, how fast they're fixed, and how serious they are. Updates in real time.

😬 Cross-City Shame Index

"Your city: D+. Next town: B+." Side-by-side rankings between neighbors. Nothing motivates a mayor like being beaten by the town next door.

🗳️ Election District Map

See which council member's district has the most unresolved issues. Know who represents you. Know their record. Vote accordingly.

💵 Budget Transparency

"Your city allocated $4.2M for roads. $1.8M spent. 340 issues remain." Follow the money — for cities that publish their budgets openly.

🔮 Predictive Analytics

Based on three years of community data, the app can guess where the next round of damage will appear. Cities can be told to fix things before they break — saving real money.

🛣️ Per-Street Decay Profiles

Each street gets its own grade and projected repair timeline. "Elm Street has a 78% chance of needing major repair within 12 months."

📰 Tools for Journalists

Local reporters can subscribe to alerts, download data exports, and pull authority performance reports. Better infrastructure stories. More accountability.

💲 Cost Estimates

Every report shows what the repair would actually cost — based on Federal Highway Administration data. Aggregate it across a city and you see the real backlog.

🎮
Engagement & Rewards

Civic engagement is hard to sustain. We borrowed a few tricks from games and habit-building apps.

🔥 Daily Streaks

Report something every day, build a streak. Seven days = 50 bonus points. Thirty days = 200 points. It feels good, and it works.

🏅 Points & Badges

Every action earns points. Points unlock badges. Badges show on your profile. Some people are motivated by saving the world; some are motivated by earning a "First 100 Reports" badge. Both groups end up reporting potholes.

🏆 Leaderboards

Global, state, and city rankings. See who's making the biggest impact in your community. Top contributors get featured.

📰 Neighborhood Feed

Real-time activity near you: new reports, fresh resolutions, milestone moments, community campaigns.

☕ Local Business Rewards

Local businesses can sponsor rewards: "Report 10 issues this month, get a free coffee." Real perks. Real participation.

📊 Weekly Digest

Every Sunday: how many reports in your area, how many got fixed, who's responding. Stay informed without opening the app every day.

🧠
Intelligence

The app gradually learns your routines and surfaces information that's actually useful to you.

🚗 Knows Your Commute

If you opt in, the app learns where you usually drive. When a pothole shows up on your morning route, it tells you — and lets you know how many more reports it needs to escalate.

⚠️ Hazard Warnings Ahead

Approaching a known hazard on your route? Your phone vibrates with a heads-up: "Caution: large pothole 200 feet ahead on your right." Crowdsourced safety.

🔔 Smart Notifications

Not spam. Context. "Your report was acknowledged." "3 more reports needed for escalation." "The city missed their deadline." You can mute everything during quiet hours.

🗺️ AR Camera Mode

Point your camera at the road. Floating markers appear over the live view, color-coded by severity, showing nearby reports. Tap any marker for details. Very Pokemon-Go-but-for-civic-engagement.

🗺️ Offline Maps

Download your area so the map works without cell service. Useful in basements, subways, rural areas, and hiking trails.

Accessibility & Languages

Built for everyone — not just tech-savvy English speakers.

🔤 Five Languages

English, Spanish, Portuguese, Chinese, and Haitian Creole — the most common languages spoken in MA, RI, and NH. Auto-detects what your phone is set to.

♿ Screen Reader Support

Every button, every label, every interaction works with VoiceOver and TalkBack. Tested with the actual tools real users rely on.

🔊 Audio-Guided Reporting

The fully voice-driven flow. The app speaks; you respond out loud. Zero screen touches. Designed for blind and low-vision users from day one.

🔠 Bigger Text? It Just Works

If you've made your phone's text bigger in system settings, Fault Line follows. Nothing gets cut off. Nothing breaks.

🌓 High Contrast Mode

Enhanced contrast colors and larger touch targets for users with low vision. Reduce-motion is honored too.
