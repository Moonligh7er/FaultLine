The Shift

Individual complaints die.
Documented evidence doesn't.

Every state in America has a "notice of defect" statute. The moment a city receives documented notification of a hazard, the legal clock starts ticking. Miss the deadline? They're financially liable for whatever happens next.

For 30 years, almost nobody has used those statutes — because almost nobody had the documentation. A 311 call isn't documentation. A photo with a timestamp, a GPS coordinate, an email trail, and a community of witnesses is.

Fault Line just makes the documentation effortless.
